Preparing a Chromecast for Use

  1. First, you’ll need to plug your Chromecast device into an HDMI port on your TV. These are typically located on the back of the TV screen.
  2. Begin charging your Chromecast by locating the cord that came with the device. Insert the appropriate ends into the Chromecast device and your TV’s USB port. This should be located somewhere near the HDMI port. A red light is now lit on your Chromecast, indicating that it is currently charging.
  3. Use your TV’s remote to change the source to the HDMI input you plugged the Chromecast into. You can now see your device’s setup name in the bottom left corner of the screen.
  4. Visit cast.google.com/chromecast/setup/ in order to download the Chromecast app and set up your preferences for the device. Click the Download button, and install the app as prompted. This can be done on either a laptop or phone/tablet.
  5. Open the app once it is installed and click Accept on the Privacy Terms page that pops up.
  6. Wait while Chromecast attempts to locate your device. Your computer should be no more than 20 feet away in order for it to be correctly detected.
  7. Click the Set Up button once Chromecast has located your device. A code appears on your laptop, phone, or tablet’s screen. It should also appear on your TV. Click ‘I see the code’ when it does.
  8. Name your Chromecast device if you would like to, then click ‘Set.’
  9. Input your Wi-Fi information into the provided form. If your Wi-Fi is password protected, input the password as well.
  10. Click ‘Finish Setup.’ Congratulations, your Chromecast is now fully prepared for use!
